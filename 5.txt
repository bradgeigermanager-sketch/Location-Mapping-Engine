Concise takeaway:  
You now get a three‑schema brane comparison system:

1. Brane Schema (updated) — a standalone, self‑contained definition of a brane (dimensions 4+).  
2. 3D Coordinates Schema (updated) — now explicitly references the brane the 3D point belongs to.  
3. Brane‑to‑Brane Comparison Schema (new) — a fixed, time‑invariant comparison object that stores:  
   - the fixed 4D separation metric,  
   - the relative distances + angles in dimensions 5+,  
   - the rates of change for those higher‑dimensional relationships,  
   - and references to the two branes being compared.

All three schemas remain atomic, DAG‑safe, dimension‑explicit, and composable.

🧬 3. Brane‑to‑Brane Comparison Schema (brane_comparison.schema.json)

- Fixed 4D separation metric

- Rates of change for those distances and angles  
- References to the two branes being compared

🧩 4. How These Schemas Work Together

A. Brane Schema
Defines the hyperspatial structure of a brane.

B. 3D Coordinates Schema
Links a 3D point to a brane.

C. Brane Comparison Schema
Defines the fixed and dynamic relationships between two branes.

DAG Structure

`
Brane A ───────┐
                ├── Brane Comparison Object
Brane B ───────┘

3D Coordinates → Brane → Hyperspatial Geometry
`

Everything is:

- Atomic  
- Composable  
- Dimension‑explicit  
- DAG‑safe  
- Extensible to 10D or more
