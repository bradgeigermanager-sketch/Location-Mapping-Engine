Below is the scenario‑level predictive system, the top of the entire LME predictive architecture.  
This is the layer that ties together:

- multiple points  
- their trajectories  
- their interactions  
- their predictions  
- their evaluations  
- their forecasting accuracy classes  
- and the scenario‑level outcome  

This is the “whole‑system” predictive intelligence layer.

⭐ 5. Scenario‑Level Algorithms

A. Scenario state vector

Stack all point states:

\[
\mathbf{S}(t) = [s1(t), s2(t), \dots, s_N(t)]^\top
\]

Each \(s_i(t)\) includes:

- 3D  
- 4D  
- 5D+ brane coordinates  

---

B. Scenario prediction

Using the multi‑point model:

\[
\mathbf{S}(t+\Delta t) = F(\mathbf{S}(t))
\]

Where \(F\) may be:

- independent  
- coupled linear  
- interaction graph  
- ML model  

---

C. Scenario error

\[
E_{\text{scenario}} =
\alpha \cdot \frac{1}{N}\sumi Ei +
\beta \cdot \frac{1}{M}\sum{(i,j)} E{ij} +
\gamma \cdot E_{\text{spread}}
\]

Where:

- \(E_i\): per‑point error  
- \(E_{ij}\): interaction error  
- \(E_{\text{spread}}\): difference in predicted vs actual scenario spread  
- \(\alpha, \beta, \gamma\): scenario‑level weights  

---

D. Scenario accuracy class assignment

Using the scenario’s total error and the accuracy profile:

\[
\text{class} = \text{AccuracyProfile}(E_{\text{scenario}})
\]
